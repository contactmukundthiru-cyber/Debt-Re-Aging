## Conference Presentation Abstract

**Title:** Automating FCRA Compliance Checks: A Free Tool for Legal Aid

**Abstract (250 words):**

Debt re-aging‚Äîthe illegal manipulation of dates on credit reports to extend the reporting period beyond statutory limits‚Äîdisproportionately affects low-income consumers who lack resources to identify and challenge these violations. Traditional analysis requires attorneys to manually calculate date differentials across multiple data points, consuming valuable time that could be spent on representation.

This presentation introduces the Credit Report Analyzer, a free tool (free for personal and nonprofit use; commercial use requires a paid license) that automates the identification of potential FCRA timeline violations. The tool processes credit report snippets using OCR technology, extracts relevant dates and account information, and applies over 10 transparent rules based on established FCRA principles. When issues are detected, it generates professional dispute documentation including bureau letters, furnisher letters, and debt validation requests.

Key features include:
- 100% local processing with no cloud data transmission
- Support for PDF and image uploads, including mobile photos
- 50-state Statute of Limitations database
- Multi-language support (English and Spanish)
- Complete audit trail of analysis and edits

Pilot results from legal aid organizations demonstrate significant time savings (averaging 1.5 hours per case) and a 25% detection rate for high-severity violations that might otherwise go unnoticed.

Attendees will learn:
1. How debt re-aging harms consumers
2. How the tool identifies potential violations
3. How to implement the tool in their organization
4. Best practices from pilot deployments

The presentation includes a live demonstration and time for Q&A.

**Keywords:** FCRA, credit reporting, legal technology, consumer protection, access to justice
